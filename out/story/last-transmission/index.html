The Last Transmission from Earth

In the year 2157, Earth sent its final message to the stars. It was not a message of hope or triumph, but a desperate plea for help from a planet that was dying. The transmission was received by the Mars Colony, the Lunar Station, and the deep space exploration vessel Aurora, but by the time they could respond, Earth was gone.

The final image of Earth before the transmission ended

My name is Dr. Elena Rodriguez, and I was the communications officer on the Aurora when we received the last transmission from Earth. What I heard that day would change my life forever, and it would set in motion a search for survivors that would span the galaxy.

The Transmission

The transmission began at 14:23 UTC on March 15th, 2157. It was a standard emergency broadcast, but the content was anything but standard. The voice on the other end was that of Dr. Marcus Chen, the director of the Global Space Agency, and he was clearly in distress.

Dr. Marcus Chen, the last person to speak from Earth

Chen's message was brief but chilling: "This is Dr. Marcus Chen, Global Space Agency. We are under attack. Repeat, we are under attack. The entity has breached our defenses. All major cities are gone. The entity is consuming everything. We are trying to evacuate, but there's not enough time. If anyone is listening, please help us. We are the last survivors of Earth."

The transmission continued for another three minutes, during which Chen described the entity that was attacking Earth. It was not a natural disaster or a military attack, but something else entirely—a being of pure energy that was consuming the planet from within.

The Entity

According to Chen's description, the entity was a massive, amorphous being of pure energy that had appeared in Earth's atmosphere without warning. It was not a physical being, but rather a force of nature that was consuming everything in its path.

The entity as it appeared in Earth's atmosphere

The entity was not just consuming matter, but also energy, time, and space itself. It was creating a void in reality, a place where the laws of physics no longer applied. As it consumed more of the planet, it grew larger and more powerful, eventually becoming a force that could not be stopped.

Chen described how the entity had first appeared as a small, dark spot in the sky, but had quickly grown to cover the entire planet. It was not just consuming the surface, but was drilling down into the planet's core, consuming everything in its path.

The Evacuation

As the entity consumed Earth, the remaining survivors attempted to evacuate. But there were not enough ships, not enough time, and not enough resources to save everyone. The evacuation was chaotic and desperate, with people fighting for the last available spaces on the few remaining ships.

Chen described how the evacuation ships were being launched from every available spaceport, but many of them were being consumed by the entity before they could escape. The entity was not just consuming the planet, but was also reaching out into space, consuming the ships that were trying to escape.

The desperate evacuation from Earth

The last ships to escape were the ones that had been launched from the most remote locations, far from the entity's reach. These ships carried the last survivors of Earth, but they were not enough to save the planet. Earth was doomed, and there was nothing anyone could do to stop it.

The Search for Survivors

After the transmission ended, the Aurora, along with the Mars Colony and the Lunar Station, began a desperate search for survivors. They searched the solar system, looking for any signs of life, but they found nothing. Earth was gone, and with it, all of its inhabitants.

The search continued for months, with the Aurora traveling to the outer reaches of the solar system and beyond. They found evidence of the entity's passage—voids in space where matter and energy had been consumed—but no survivors.

The search for survivors in the vastness of space

The search was not just about finding survivors, but also about understanding what had happened to Earth. The crew of the Aurora studied the entity's effects on space and time, trying to understand how it had consumed an entire planet.

The New Home

After months of searching, the crew of the Aurora found a new home—a planet in a distant star system that was capable of supporting human life. The planet was not Earth, but it was close enough to be called home.

The planet was named New Earth, and it became the new home for the survivors of Earth. The crew of the Aurora, along with the survivors from the Mars Colony and the Lunar Station, began the process of building a new civilization.

New Earth, the new home for the survivors

The process of building a new civilization was not easy. The survivors had to start from scratch, building everything from the ground up. But they were determined to make it work, and they were determined to honor the memory of those who had been lost on Earth.

The Legacy

The last transmission from Earth became a symbol of hope and determination for the survivors. It was a reminder of what they had lost, but also a reminder of what they had gained—a new home, a new beginning, and a new chance to build a better future.

The transmission was preserved and studied, becoming a part of the new civilization's history. It was taught to children as a lesson about the importance of unity and cooperation, and it was used as a reminder of the fragility of life and the importance of protecting what we have.

The legacy of Earth's last transmission
